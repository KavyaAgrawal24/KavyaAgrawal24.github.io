## Groundwater Contamination Modelling

My doctoral work focuses on developing scalable geospatial AI frameworks for predicting groundwater contamination across India.

## Uranium Contamination Risk Mapping

This work integrates uranium observations, hydrogeochemical variables, remote sensing indicators, terrain, climate, land-use, population, and settlement datasets to predict uranium contamination probability and assess spatial risk.

Key components include:

- Uranium probability mapping
- Transition regime identification
- Population exposure assessment
- Urban and peri-urban exposure analysis
- Governance blind spot diagnostics
- Policy priority zoning

## Fluoride Deep Learning Benchmarking

This work compares deep learning model performance across multiple spatial resolutions to understand how scale affects groundwater contamination prediction.

## Arsenic Transferability and Interpretability

This research direction focuses on temporal transferability, robustness, and explainable AI for arsenic contamination modelling.

## Exposure and Governance Diagnostics

Beyond prediction, I work on translating risk maps into policy-relevant indicators such as:

- Monitoring Efficiency Index
- Governance Blind Spot Index
- Exposure Inequality Index
- Transition Sensitivity Index
- Composite Priority Index
