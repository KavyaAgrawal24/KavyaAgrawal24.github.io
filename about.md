  <h1>About Me</h1>
  <p class="lead">
    I am a Ph.D. Scholar at TERI School of Advanced Studies, Department of Natural & Applied Sciences.
  </p>
</div>

I work at the intersection of groundwater science, remote sensing, GIS, artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, and environmental policy. My doctoral research focuses on groundwater contamination mapping and prediction across India, with emphasis on scalable geospatial modelling frameworks that can support risk-informed monitoring and decision-making.

My work integrates multi-source and multi-resolution geospatial datasets, including hydrogeochemical observations, climate variables, terrain attributes, land use/land cover, soil properties, population datasets, settlement layers, and remotely sensed indicators. I am particularly interested in how data-driven models can move beyond prediction to support interpretation, uncertainty assessment, exposure analysis, and policy prioritization.

Beyond my core work on groundwater contamination, I am interested in interdisciplinary connections between hydrogeology, climate change, cryospheric studies, archaeology, history, urban systems, and public health.
