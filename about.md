    <h1>About Me</h1>
    <p class="lead">I am a Ph.D. Scholar in Natural & Applied Sciences at TERI School of Advanced Studies, New Delhi.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="prose">
    <p>My work sits at the intersection of groundwater science, remote sensing, GIS, artificial intelligence, environmental health, and policy-oriented spatial analysis.</p>
    <p>I am particularly interested in developing scalable frameworks that integrate hydrogeochemical observations, remotely sensed datasets, climatic variables, terrain characteristics, land-use information, and socio-environmental indicators to model groundwater contamination across India.</p>
    <p>My doctoral research focuses on building adaptable geospatial AI frameworks for predicting contamination probability, understanding environmental controls, assessing uncertainty, and translating model outputs into exposure and governance-relevant indicators.</p>
    <h2>Seeking Opportunities</h2>
    <p>I am actively interested in interdisciplinary research and training opportunities in groundwater contamination, cryospheric sciences, remote sensing, environmental health, climate systems, and geospatial policy analysis.</p>
    <p>Beyond groundwater contamination modelling, I am deeply interested in interdisciplinary approaches connecting hydrogeology, cryospheric studies, environmental history, archaeology, climate systems, and spatial decision-making.</p>
